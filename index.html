We provide the best process experience

Preparing the Frame:

The first step involves creating the frame of the stool. This typically involves shaping and assembling wooden or metal components to form the basic structure of the stool. The frame is designed to provide stability and support for the stool. Precise measurements and cuts are made to ensure the frame is sturdy and well-balanced.

Weaving Rattan Strands:

Once the frame is ready, the next step is to weave rattan strands around it to form the seat and backrest of the stool. Rattan, a flexible and durable natural material, is often used for this purpose. Skilled artisans weave the rattan strands in intricate patterns, using techniques such as traditional cane weaving or modern weaving methods. The weaving process requires patience and precision to achieve a uniform and aesthetically pleasing finish.

Finishing Touches:

After the rattan weaving is complete, the final step involves adding any additional finishing touches to the stool. This may include sanding and smoothing the surfaces, applying a protective finish such as varnish or lacquer to enhance durability and appearance, and attaching any additional features such as footrests or decorative elements. Once all the finishing touches are in place, the rattan stool is ready for use or for further customization according to the manufacturer's specifications or customer preferences.
